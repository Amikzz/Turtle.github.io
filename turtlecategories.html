Turtle Categories

1. Green Turtle


Sri Lankan waters are home to the Green Turtle, which is the most commonly found species in the region. These turtles can grow up to 1 meter in length and weigh around 250 kg. Adult female Green Turtles can lay anywhere between 120 to 140 eggs at a time. Also referred to as the green sea turtle, black turtle, or Pacific green turtle, this species can gracefully glide through waters in regions where sea temperatures don't drop below 68 Fahrenheit. The Green Turtle can be seen in the coastal waters of over 140 countries, including Sri Lanka, where it can be found in Rekawa.

2. Hawksbill Turtle


The Hawksbill Turtle, which is smaller than the Green Turtle, is comparatively rarer and is currently classified as critically endangered. These turtles have a maximum length of 90cm and weigh around 50 to 70kg. They are named after their narrow head and bird-like beak, which is used to catch prey hiding in small crevices. Despite being smaller, the Hawksbill Turtle is famous for its stunning shell, which comprises 13 symmetrical and colorful pieces. These turtles can be found in Bundala and Kosgoda, among other locations.

3. Loggerhead Turtles


Loggerhead Turtles are easily identifiable due to their large head and their distinctive red and brown coloration. These turtles can grow up to 1 meter in size and weigh around 170 to 200kg. They are primarily carnivorous and use their muscular jaws to crush their favorite food, mollusks, and crustaceans. Compared to the Green Sea Turtle, Loggerheads are larger and omnivorous, while Green Turtles are herbivorous and smaller in size. In Sri Lanka, Loggerhead Turtles are rare, but they have been spotted on the beaches of Kosgoda, Rekawa, and Bundala.

Where to find Loggerhead Turtles in Sri lanka?



4. Leatherback Turtle


The Leatherback Turtle, which is the largest among the five species found in Sri Lanka, is critically endangered and on the verge of extinction. This turtle is easily recognizable due to its long front flippers and distinctive black and white stripy shell. Unlike other turtles, the Leatherback's shell is a layer of thin, tough, rubbery skin peppered with thousands of bone plates, giving it a leathery appearance. It is the only sea turtle without a hard shell. Leatherbacks have been spotted on rare occasions on Sri Lankan shores, particularly around Kosgoda and Rekawa Beach.

Where to find Leatherback Turtles in Sri lanka?



5. Olive Ridley Turtle


The smallest of them all, Olive Ridley, is the cutest (of course in comparison to others). The Olive Ridley is the smallest species in the nation, growing to a maximum size of 65 cm and weighing just 35 to 45 kg. Its carapace, which has an olive/rust tint, inspired its name. The Pacific ridley sea turtle is another name for the olive ridley sea turtle. Out of all the sea turtles found worldwide, they are the most prevalent. This species is known to lay its eggs in the Sri Lankan locations of Karaduwa, Maggona, Bundala, Marichchikaddi, Kalpitiya, Udappuwa, Hambantota, Kundakuliya, Chilaw, Negambo, Matara, and Weligama.
